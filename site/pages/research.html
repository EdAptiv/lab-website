Adaptive Learning Algorithms

We developed the adaptive algorithm behind Prowise Learn (formerly Math Garden), a widely used platform for practicing math, language, logic, and typing. Inspired by the Elo rating system, this work bridges education and research, leveraging over a decade of large-scale practice data to support studies in psychology and learning science.

Error Tracing

Our work in error tracing involves sophisticated analysis of student mistakes to diagnose underlying misconceptions. By identifying patterns in errors, we aim to provide precise, actionable feedback that helps learners overcome specific difficulties and deepen their understanding.

Motivation

We investigate psychological factors influencing learner motivation, including self-efficacy, goal orientation, and intrinsic interest. Our studies explore how adaptive systems and pedagogical interventions can foster sustained engagement and a positive attitude towards learning.

Formal Models of Learning

Our team develops mathematical and computational models to describe and predict learning processes. These formal models provide a rigorous framework for understanding how knowledge is acquired, retained, and applied, informing the design of more effective educational tools.

Learning & Cognition

This area explores the fundamental cognitive processes underlying learning, including memory, attention, problem-solving, and decision-making. We apply insights from cognitive psychology to design educational interventions that align with how the human mind learns most effectively.

Math Anxiety

Our research addresses the pervasive issue of math anxiety, investigating its causes, effects on learning and performance, and effective intervention strategies. We aim to develop educational approaches that reduce anxiety and promote positive mathematical identities in learners.
